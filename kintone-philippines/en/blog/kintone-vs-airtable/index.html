The Airtable Record Limit Problem

Airtable's 50,000 record limit per base is the #1 reason businesses switch to Kintone. Once you hit this ceiling, your options are limited:

  • Pay for Enterprise (very expensive)
  • Split data across multiple bases (messy)
  • Archive old records (lose history)
  • Switch to Kintone (unlimited records)

Workflow Automation Comparison

Kintone includes built-in process management with approval workflows, status transitions, automatic notifications, and assignee routing. You can design complex multi-step workflows without add-ons.

Airtable offers basic automations (triggers and actions) but lacks true process management. Complex workflows require external tools like Zapier, adding cost and complexity.

When to Choose Kintone

  • You have or will exceed 50,000 records
  • You need approval workflows and process management
  • Enterprise security compliance is required
  • You want JavaScript customization for advanced features
  • You need local support in the Philippines
  • You're building multiple interconnected business apps

When to Choose Airtable

  • You're a small team with under 50,000 records
  • You need a simple, spreadsheet-like interface
  • Budget is extremely limited (free plan)
  • You only need basic database functionality
  • Your team is already familiar with Airtable

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Kintone better than Airtable?

Kintone is better for businesses needing unlimited records, workflow automation, and enterprise security. Airtable is simpler for small teams with basic needs.

What is the Airtable record limit?

Airtable limits you to 50,000 records per base on Team/Business plans, 500,000 on Enterprise. Kintone has no record limits.

Can I import Airtable data to Kintone?

Yes! Export from Airtable as CSV and import directly into Kintone. Edamame Inc. offers free migration assistance.
