😤 Common Challenges in Education Software

Philippine education software companies face unique operational challenges that generic software can't solve. Here's what we hear from businesses every day:

Paper-Heavy Enrollment

Parents queuing for hours to submit physical enrollment forms. Staff manually encoding data into systems. Lost documents requiring re-submission every school year.

Fragmented Student Records

Grades in one system, attendance in another, discipline records on paper. No unified student profile that teachers, counselors, and administrators can access.

Manual Report Generation

Staff spending weeks compiling DepEd/CHED-required reports by hand from multiple data sources. Error-prone and always late.

Communication Gaps

Important announcements reaching parents through unreliable channels. No systematic way to communicate grades, attendance issues, or school events. For more details, see our guide to HR management for educational staff.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can Kintone generate DepEd EBEIS reports?

Yes. Kintone can be configured to export data in the exact format required by DepEd's EBEIS/LEARNIS system. This turns a weeks-long manual process into a one-click export.

Is it suitable for both K-12 and higher education?

Absolutely. Kintone's flexibility means it can be configured for K-12 (DepEd grading, quarterly reports) or higher education (CHED compliance, credit-based systems, thesis tracking) with equal ease.

Can parents access student information?

Yes. Set up parent portals with read-only access to grades, attendance, billing statements, and school announcements. Parents get a dedicated login with access only to their child's data.

Does it support online/blended learning?

Kintone can serve as a learning management hub—distributing materials, collecting assignments, and tracking completion. It integrates with video conferencing platforms for a complete blended learning solution.

How affordable is it for schools with tight budgets?

Kintone starts from ₱1,000/user/month. Small schools can start with just administrative staff (5-10 users), then expand as budget allows. It's far more affordable than dedicated school management software.
